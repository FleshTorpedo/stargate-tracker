The Expanded Library

Stargate's print and panel canon is vast, and fans famously argue over where it all slots in. The screen order above stays clean; here is where the major book and comic lines anchor, plus the side material. None of it is required — but for a true completionist, this is the map.

📕 Post-film branch after the movie

Bill McCay — Rebellion · Retaliation · Retribution · Reconnaissance · Resistance (Roc, 1995–99)

Five novels continuing the 1994 film from Emmerich's own notes. An alternate continuity that SG-1 overwrites — here Sha're is "Sha'uri," there is no Carter or Teal'c, and the program goes public. Read as a glorious "what could have been."

📗 Post-film branch after the movie

Entity Comics — One Nation Under Ra · Underworld (1996–97, collected as The New Adventures Collection)

The other pre-TV continuation: a film adaptation plus original stories with new characters. Its own non-canon branch, parallel to McCay.

📘 SG-1 tie-ins across seasons 1–10

Roc / Ashley McConnell — Sacrifice Moon (the team's literal first mission, ~S1), The Price You Pay, The First Amendment, The Morpheus Factor.

Fandemonium — 30+ SG-1 novels & novellas set throughout the run; read each within its matching season. (e.g. Behind Enemy Lines follows S7's "Fragile Balance.")

Comics — Avatar Press (2003–07) and Dynamite's Vala & Daniel minis (2010–11), all set during the series.

📘 Atlantis tie-ins across seasons 1–5

Fandemonium — the Rising novelization plus a long run of Pegasus adventures (Reliquary, Casualties of War, Death Game, Mirror, Mirror…), placed within their seasons.

Comics — Avatar Press Atlantis (2005–07).

📕 Atlantis "Season 6" after SGA finale

Legacy series — Homecoming · The Lost · Allegiance · The Furies · Secrets · Inheritors (then Unascended & The Third Path).

The beloved book-form continuation, picking up the instant "Enemy at the Gate" ends. The closest thing to an official next chapter for Atlantis.

📗 Post-finale comics after SGA / SGU

American Mythology — Atlantis: Back to Pegasus · Gateways · Hearts & Minds · Singularity. A separate post-S5 continuation that diverges from the Legacy novels — enjoy either as its own timeline.

American Mythology — Universe: Back to Destiny resolves the "Gauntlet" cliffhanger the show never could, plus the SGA/SGU Anthology one-shots.

📘 Universe in print

Fandemonium — Stargate Universe: Air, the novelization of the three-part pilot. The lone SGU novel.

⊘ Outside canon — watch anytime

Stargate Infinity (animated, 26 eps, 2002–03) — aired during SG-1's sixth season with no involvement from the live-action team, and it contradicts established lore. Set in the future; elements stop fitting after SG-1 Season 5.

Games — SG-1: Unleashed, Stargate Resistance, and the cancelled MMO Stargate Worlds. Fun side material, not part of the story.

How this order was built. The spine is GateWorld's Optimized Stargate Watch Order — broadcast order, but with multi-part stories kept in one block and a couple of intended continuity fixes restored (notably SG-1 "Covenant" before "Affinity," and the Atlantis Season 3 finale held until after The Ark of Truth). It is cross-referenced against the watch guides at ScreenRant, SlashFilm, ComicBook.com and Popverse — which all converge on the same interleaving — with expanded-universe placements drawn from the Stargate Wiki and publisher listings.

337 checkpoints span the full saga: all 354 episodes across SG-1, Atlantis and Universe, four films, and the Origins prequel. Two- and three-part stories are grouped as single stops. Your progress saves automatically. The ninth chevron — like Destiny's own address — completes the connection.
